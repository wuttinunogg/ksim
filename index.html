02 · Problem Statement
연구 문제 · Anecdote vs. Benchmark
The Problem 문제 진술

Korean excellence
enters Thai practice
as anecdote, not
as a benchmark.

일화에서 벤치마크로 From anecdote to benchmark — the shift this dissertation proposes.

Thai practitioners admire Korean brands and cite them constantly — yet Korean cases enter Thai management thinking anecdotally, not systematically. As outputs to be imitated, not as systems to be learned from.

The Thai-authored literature on Korean brand competitiveness is thin. Southeast Asian comparative perspectives are under-represented internationally. The result: Thai organizations imitate surface form without understanding underlying system — producing predictably inconsistent results.

This dissertation closes that gap through a rigorous, Thai-standpoint, scholarly-anchored benchmarking study.

ii.
이 단계
Thai Comparative
Application
Qualitative · Comparative Application

Matched case design · Analytic, Not Mechanical

The Phase 1 framework is applied to Thai cases matched analytically — on sector and strategic position. The objective is disciplined comparison, not forced equivalence between non-identical national contexts.

Bounded institutional case study · UTCC

A privileged empirical site: the researcher's senior role at UTCC provides legitimate, continuous access to strategic decisions, campaign data, and institutional dilemmas rarely available to external researchers. A documented firewall between administrative authority and research recruitment manages the dual role.

Practitioner interviews · n ≈ 15–20

Designed to surface the strategic logics, institutional constraints, and cultural sense-making through which Thai professionals encounter, adopt, or resist Korean-derived practices.

Quantitative · Transferability Testing

Instrument development · Two-Stage

Survey instrument derived from Phase 1–2 findings. Initial draft piloted with ≈ 30 practitioners, refined, then administered to a stratified sample of ≈ 300–400 Thai marketing practitioners across sectors.

What the survey measures · Four Dimensions

Familiarity & Adoption Perceived Fit Reported Outcomes Contextual Moderators

Analytical approach · Integrated

Scale validation (CFA) · Regression for main effects · Moderation analysis for boundary conditions · Structural equation modelling where sample size permits · Integrated joint-display analysis combining qualitative and quantitative findings around shared constructs.
